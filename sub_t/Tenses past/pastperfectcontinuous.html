PAST PERFECT CONTINUOUS

¿Qué es el pasado perfecto continuo?

El pasado perfecto continuo (Past Perfect Continuous) es un tiempo verbal en inglés que se utiliza para hablar de acciones que comenzaron en el pasado y continuaron hasta otro momento también en el pasado. Describe acciones prolongadas que tuvieron una duración antes de otro punto del pasado.

  • Enfatiza la duración o continuidad de una acción en el pasado.
  • Conecta dos momentos diferentes del pasado.

Objetivo

El objetivo del pasado perfecto continuo es expresar la duración de una acción que comenzó y continuó hasta otro momento del pasado, a menudo destacando el esfuerzo, la duración o los resultados de esa acción.

  • Explicar cuánto tiempo duró una acción antes de otra.
  • Mostrar causas de situaciones en el pasado.
  • Enfatizar el aspecto continuo de una acción pasada.

Usos principales

  • Acciones que duraron hasta otro momento del pasado: She had been studying for three hours when her friend arrived.
  • Causas de situaciones pasadas: He was tired because he had been working all night.
  • Acciones repetidas antes de un punto en el pasado: They had been trying to contact him for weeks before he finally answered.
  • Efectos o resultados visibles en el pasado: When I saw him, his eyes were red because he had been crying.

Fundamento teórico y estructura

  • Formación:
    • Auxiliar had + been + verbo principal en forma -ing.
  • Estructura:
    • Afirmativa: Sujeto + had been + verbo-ing + complemento.
      I had been waiting for two hours when she finally arrived.
    • Negativa: Sujeto + had not (hadn't) been + verbo-ing + complemento.
      They had not been living there for long when the accident happened.
    • Interrogativa: Had + sujeto + been + verbo-ing + complemento?
      Had you been working all night?
  • Expresiones de tiempo frecuentes: for two hours, since morning, all day, before that time, how long.

Diferencias con otros tiempos verbales

  • Pasado continuo: Describe una acción en progreso en un momento específico del pasado.
    I was reading when she called.
  • Pasado perfecto: Describe una acción que se completó antes de otra acción en el pasado.
    I had finished dinner before he arrived.
  • Pasado perfecto continuo: Enfatiza la duración de una acción hasta un punto en el pasado.
    I had been reading for two hours when she called.

Ejemplos adicionales

  • She had been teaching for ten years before she became a principal.
  • He had been playing football when he injured his knee.
  • The ground was wet because it had been raining all night.
  • I hadn't been sleeping well before the exam.
  • Had they been waiting long before the bus arrived?

Errores frecuentes

  • Olvidar el auxiliar "been": She had working... ❌ → She had been working... ✔️
  • Usar con verbos estáticos: I had been knowing him... ❌ → I had known him... ✔️
  • Confundirlo con el pasado perfecto: I had eaten... ❌ (si queremos expresar duración) → I had been eating... ✔️
  • Olvidar que la forma negativa se forma con "had not been": I hadn't working... ❌ → I hadn't been working... ✔️

Ejercicios prácticos

  1. Completa: She was tired because she ______ (study) all night.
  2. Haz la pregunta: They had been playing tennis for two hours.
  3. Haz la negativa: I had been waiting for you.
  4. Traduce: Ellos habían estado viviendo allí durante diez años.
Ver respuestas correctas
  • had been studying
  • Had they been playing tennis for two hours?
  • I had not (hadn't) been waiting for you.
  • They had been living there for ten years.

Notas importantes

  • El pasado perfecto continuo no se usa con verbos de estado (know, believe, love...).
  • Se utiliza principalmente para enfatizar la duración de una acción antes de otro momento del pasado.
  • Siempre debe incluir "had been" seguido de la forma -ing del verbo.
  • A menudo se usa con "for" o "since" para indicar el período de tiempo.
